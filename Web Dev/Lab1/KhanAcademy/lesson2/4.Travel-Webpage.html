Semey

Semey, located in eastern Kazakhstan, is a city with a rich history and cultural heritage. Formerly known as Semipalatinsk, it played a significant role during the Soviet era, particularly as a major nuclear testing site. Today, Semey is a vibrant urban center, offering a mix of modern amenities and historical landmarks. The city is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including the Irtysh River, enhancing its natural beauty. Semey is also known for its diverse population, reflecting the multicultural fabric of Kazakhstan.
